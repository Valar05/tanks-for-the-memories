# Information Pressure Report

This report collects repeated battlefield and life-system patterns where information was missing, delayed, wrong, contradictory, or changed the outcome.

## Missing Information
- Omaha Beachhead: radios were lost and exits stayed unopened, so local commanders lacked a coherent picture.
- Carentan: artillery communications were unavailable at the decisive moment.
- Daily life sources show that soldiers also lacked stable sleep, food variety, and clean conditions.

## Delayed Information
- Cross-Channel Attack, Chapter IX: reports reached headquarters late and scattered.
- Military mail and radio procedure sources show why structured messages still take time to transmit and process.

## Wrong Information
- Cross-Channel Attack, Chapter VIII: German intelligence was blind or misled about invasion timing.
- Operation Cobra: expectations about weather and bombing did not match the reality of the first strike.

## Contradictory Information
- Omaha Beachhead and Heinrich Severloh provide incompatible local pictures of the same fight.
- Operation Totalize and coalition sources show that air/ground views can diverge from ground reality.
- Occupation sources can disagree about who controls a town and what that control means.

## Changed Outcomes
- Operation Cobra: weather delay and rhino modification changed whether the bocage became a stall or a breakout.
- Villers-Bocage: route selection and surprise overrode raw vehicle quality.
- Carentan: missing artillery communications and the failure to pass orders altered the attack’s shape.
- Mail latency and fatigue changed small-unit behavior even away from direct fire.

## Reusable Patterns
- Visible certainty is rare; reports are partial and age quickly.
- Terrain can become a communications problem.
- A commander who waits for confirmation can still be wrong if the channel is broken.
- Repair, supply, and mail are information systems in disguise.
