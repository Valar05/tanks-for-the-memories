# Surprising Discoveries

- Firefly crews visually disguised the 17-pounder barrel. The gun itself changed the tank's information profile: it was a heavy-kill tank that also needed to look like a normal Sherman.
- Normandy crews solved hedgerow fighting with the Rhino / prongs family. This is not a cosmetic mod; it is a terrain hardpoint.
- Sherman DD turns amphibious launch into a pre-contact state. The tank is not fully a tank until the screen is dropped and the crew believes the beach picture.
- Wet stowage is a survivability module, not a flavor note. It changes how the crew values ammo placement and when they feel safe enough to carry rounds.
- M18 Hellcat and M36 Jackson show that sightlines are a design resource. Open tops are a visibility upgrade with a protection cost.
- Churchill specialist variants prove that the turret is not the whole vehicle. AVRE and Crocodile are vehicle-family overlays, not new tanks.
- M32 recovery vehicles reveal a separate rear-area hardpoint loop: tow, lift, repair, and return to service are gameplay, not bookkeeping.
- Tank destroyer doctrine often assumes ambush and reserve use, but Normandy forced many TDs into direct-fire support. The architecture needs room for doctrine violations.
- Commonwealth and U.S. radios are not interchangeable flavor. Radio family changes command latency, crew roles, and who can safely be the observer.
- Identification mistakes are inevitable. A component system should let the player misread a Panther-shaped contact before better optics, better range, or better crew skill correct it.
