# Simulation Notes

The corpus now supports a WWII life-and-command simulation where information quality, timing, sustainment, and social pressure drive orders and consequences.
The query layer exposes this as structured answers with markdown and JSON output.

## Package Layers
- Combat: tactics, visibility, radio, vehicles, and command.
- Daily life: sleep, food, hygiene, boredom, mail, and discipline.
- Logistics: fuel, ammo, maintenance, recovery, transport, and spare parts.
- Civilian context: liberation, occupation, displacement, and administrative burden.
- Psychology: fatigue, stress, memory, hesitation, confidence, and grief.

## Representative Sources
- Omaha Beachhead: beach chaos, missing radios, and fragmentary command.
- Army Service Forces: sustainment, transport, medical care, and repair.
- Military mail: censorship and home contact as morale infrastructure.
- Military government: occupation and civilian authority after combat.
- Vehicle recovery: disabled vehicles as recoverable states, not immediate loss.
